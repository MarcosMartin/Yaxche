Why Costa Rica

The case for Costa Rica

Land banking is the practice of securing land for the resources it holds and sustains. Well-informed families have used it for generations to build lasting wealth. Costa Rica has the conditions this strategy needs, and institutional capital has not yet arrived.

∿

Freshwater Security

Year-round rainfall and renewable river systems. Water is now a traded financial instrument (CME Group, NQH2O). Land with embedded freshwater is becoming a strategic resource.

⚖

Legal Stability

Continuous democracy since 1949. Military abolished constitutionally. Full property rights for foreign nationals, identical to citizens. Clear title through the Registro Nacional.

◈

Agricultural Productivity

Volcanic soils and year-round growing seasons support specialty coffee, cacao, tropical fruits, and timber. No off-season. The land produces continuously.

△

Climate Resilience

Elevation buffers against sea-level rise, extreme heat, and hurricane risk. Stable year-round temperate climate. Ranked 58th globally on the 2024 Global Peace Index.

How We Work

How we work

Yaxche does not simply present opportunities. We manage the full pathway from first introduction through to closing, supported by a team with deep roots in Costa Rica and international transaction experience.

1

Introduction and Qualification

We present verified, off-market land opportunities in Costa Rica to qualified international buyers. All opportunities are independently assessed before presentation.

2

Legal and Structural Review

Our Costa Rican legal counsel conducts full title verification, ownership structure review, and advises on the most appropriate acquisition structure for the buyer's jurisdiction and objectives.

3

Tax and Residency Advisory

We guide buyers through the full tax picture in Costa Rica and in coordination with their home jurisdiction. For buyers pursuing Investor Residency, immigration counsel is engaged in parallel.

4

Transaction and Closing

We coordinate the full transaction process, from offer and negotiation through to title transfer and registration. The buyer has a single point of contact throughout.
